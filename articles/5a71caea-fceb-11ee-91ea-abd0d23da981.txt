              <div class="my-4">
                <p class="text-lg text-justify">
                    The Tejas LCA is a single-engine, single-seat supersonic fighter that is lightweight and agile.
                    It is a symbol of India's development in aeronautical technology, designed and built by the Indian Ministry of 
                    Defence's Aeronautical Development Agency (ADA), with Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) serving as the primary industrial contractor. 
                    Tejas deliveries to the IAF are intended to replace the IAF's outmoded and aging fighters, mainly MiG-21s. However,
                    it would be introduced as part of IAF modernization rather than as a replacement for the MiG-21. 
                </p>
              </div>
              <br>
              <div class="my-8">
                <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold">
                    Evolution: The Ascend of Tejas via MK1 to MK1-A.
                </h2>
              </div>
              <div>
                <p class="text-lg text-justify my-1">
                    The Tejas MK1 was designed to meet the Indian Air Force's (IAF) need for a modern, 
                    lightweight, and agile fighter aircraft. With its advanced avionics and weaponry systems, 
                    the Tejas MK1 is capable of conducting air superiority, ground attack, and reconnaissance missions with ease. 
                    Its versatility and maneuverability make it a formidable asset in any combat scenario.
                </p>
                <p class="text-lg text-justify my-2">
                    The evolution of the Tejas MK1 has been a long and challenging journey. The development of 
                    the aircraft has faced numerous setbacks and delays, but the dedicated team of engineers and scientists
                     at ADA and HAL have persevered and overcome the obstacles in their path.
                     The aircraft has undergone extensive testing and evaluation to ensure that it meets the stringent 
                     requirements of the Indian Air Force. As the Tejas MK1 continues to evolve, with upgrades and improvements 
                     being made to enhance its capabilities and performance, it is set to become a key asset in the IAF's fleet. 
                </p>
            </div>
            <div class="my-4">
                <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold">Important Events in Tejas Journey</h3>
            </div>
            <div class="my-8">
                <div class="timeline bg-blue-900 text-white p-6 shadow-lg">
                    <ul>
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">1984</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">Establishment of ADA</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">Government of India established Aeronautical Development Agency (ADA) under the aegis of Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) to manage the LCA programme. The ADA was entrusted with the design and development of LCA while HAL was chosen as the principal contractor.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">1990</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">Design of the Tejas Aircraft Finalised</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">The design of the Tejas was finalised in 1990 as a small tailless compound delta wing design with relaxed static stability. To provide enhanced manoeuvrability it would be a control configured vehicle incorporating a digital FBW flight control system.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">1992</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">CLAW Team by  National Aerospace Laboratories</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">The CLAW team had to completed the design and integration of the flight control laws with the flight control system software, with the aid of an Iron Bird test rig.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">2001</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">First Flight of Prototype TD-1</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">On 4 January 2001, on its maiden flight, the TD-1 successfully flew with an indigenous quadruplex digital FBW flight control system.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">2011</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">Initial Operation Clearance Granted</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">The Tejas was awarded initial operational clearance-I (IOC-I) in January 2011. To ease up the process of FOC, an interim IOC-II was issued to Tejas in December 2013. </p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">2016</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">Official Induction of IOC-II (FOC) cleared Tejas in Indian Airforce </h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">The first squadron, consisting of Tejas in IOC-II configuration, became operational in 2016. The No. 45 Squadron IAF based at Sulur Air Force Station, Coimbatore was the first to have their MiG-21s replaced by Tejas aircraft at the base.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                        <!-- Add more list items here -->
                        <li class="relative py-4 cursor-pointer transition duration-500 hover:bg-blue-800">
                            <span class="mx-4 inline-block bg-blue-600 rounded-full py-1 px-3 text-sm">2019</span>
                            <div class=" mx-4 content mt-2">
                                <h3 class="text-blue-300 text-lg">Final Operation Clearance Granted for Production</h3>
                                <p class="text-blue-200">On 20 February 2019, during the Aero India 2019 show, FOC was formally awarded to the Tejas. The second Tejas squadron No.18 Flying Bullets was formed at Sulur Air Force Station on 27 May 2020 with the first four serial production FOC aircraft.</p>
                            </div>
                        </li>

                    </ul>

              <div class="my-8">
                <h3 class="text-2xl ">
                  Operational Varients of Tejas
                </h3>
                <p>
                  These Varients of Tejas, are slowly taking over of Indian Airforce's Point Defence capabilities 
                  and will become backbone of Indian Airforce after completion of delivery of planes. currently,
                  Indian Airforce already has 40  MK-1 Standard planes and already ordered 83 Mk-1A Standard Planes.
                  And it is expected that Airforce will place follow-on order of 97 Planes also. 
                </p>
              </div>
              <div>
                <ul>
                  <li class="my-2">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold">
                      Tejas MK-1
                    </h3>
                    <p class="text-justify">
                      The Tejas MK-1 is the initial operational variant of the Tejas light combat aircraft developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) for the Indian Air Force (IAF).
                      It has been in service with the IAF since July 2016 and has undergone several upgrades and modifications to enhance its capabilities.
                      The Tejas MK-1 is primarily used for air defense, ground attack, and reconnaissance missions in the Indian Air Force.
                    </p>
                    <div class="mx-4">
                      <p class="text-lg font-bold">
                        Capablities:
                      </p>
                      <ol>
                        <li>Elta EL/M-2032 radar</li>
                        <li>Hands On Throttle And Stick (HOTAS) controls</li>
                        <li>Hardpoints: 8 (3× under-wing, 1× under-fuselage, 2× wingtip rails) </li>
                        <li>Max takeoff weight: 13,500 kilograms (29,762 lb)</li>
                        <li>Combat radius: 300 kilometers</li>
                        <li>Helmet-mounted display and sight system</li>
                      </ol>
                    </div>

                  </li>
                  <li class="my-2">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold">
                      Tejas MK-1A
                    </h3>
                    <p class="text-justify">
                      The Tejas Mk-1A variant is an upgraded version of the Tejas Mk-1 developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) for the Indian Air Force (IAF).
                      It is expected to address various shortcomings and incorporate enhancements over the Mk-1 variant, including improved radar, avionics, and weapons capabilities.
                      The Tejas Mk-1A is anticipated to play a significant role in the modernization efforts of the Indian Air Force, providing enhanced indigenous combat capability.
                    </p>
                    <div class="mx-4">
                      <p class="text-lg font-bold">
                        Capablities:
                      </p>
                      <ol>
                        <li>Uttam AESA radar</li>
                        <li>Hands On Throttle And Stick (HOTAS) controls</li>
                        <li>Hardpoints: 8 (3× under-wing, 1× under-fuselage, 2× wingtip rails) </li>
                        <li>Max takeoff weight: 13,500 kilograms (29,762 lb)</li>
                        <li>Combat radius: 500 kilometers</li>
                        <li>Helmet-mounted display and sight system</li>
                        <li>Integrated electronic warfare suite</li>
                      </ol>
                    </div>
                  </li>
                </ul>
